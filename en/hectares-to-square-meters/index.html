How many square metres are in a hectare? Area unit converter

One hectare is 10,000 square metres (square meters, in US spelling): a square measuring 100 × 100 metres. With the converter above you can turn any number of hectares into square metres, square kilometres, acres, square miles, square feet or square yards — and the other way around, from any of those units back into hectares. And if you want to actually see how big that area is, the Hectareometer draws it to scale on a real map.

Frequently asked questions about hectares and square metres

How many square metres are in a hectare?
One hectare is 10,000 square metres: a square measuring 100 × 100 metres. That is 0.01 km², or about 2.47 acres.
How many hectares are in a square kilometre?
One square kilometre is 100 hectares, i.e. 1,000,000 square metres.
How many acres is a hectare?
One hectare is 2.47105 acres. The other way around, one international acre is 4,046.86 square metres, roughly 0.4 hectares.
Is a hectare the same as a football field?
Not quite: a standard football pitch (105 × 68 metres) is about 7,140 square metres, i.e. 0.71 hectares. One hectare is almost a pitch and a half.
